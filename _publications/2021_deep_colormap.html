--- layout: publication title: "Deep Colormap Extraction from Visualizations" authors: - Linping Yuan - Wei Zeng - Siwei Fu - Zhiliang Zeng - Haotian Li - Chi-Wing Fu - Huamin Qu year: 2021 venue: IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics venue_tags: - TVCG type: - Journal tags: - Color - Visualization - AI for X highlight: true doi: 10.1109/TVCG.2021.3070876 image: /assets/paper_teaser/2021_deep_colormap.png pdf: /assets/paper/2021_deep_colormap.pdf appendix: /assets/appendix/2021_deep_colormap_appendix.pdf code: https://github.com/yuanlinping/deep_colormap_extraction arxiv: 2103.00741 --- This work presents a new approach based on deep learning to automatically extract colormaps from visualizations. After summarizing colors in an input visualization image as a Lab color histogram, we pass the histogram to a pre-trained deep neural network, which learns to predict the colormap that produces the visualization. To train the network, we create a new dataset of âˆ¼64K visualizations that cover various data distributions, chart types, and colormaps. The network adopts an atrous spatial pyramid pooling module to capture color features at multiple scales in the input color histograms. We then classify the predicted colormap as discrete or continuous, and refine the predicted colormap based on its color histogram.
